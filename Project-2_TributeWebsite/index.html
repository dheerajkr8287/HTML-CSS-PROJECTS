Dr A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missle Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam: From Missile Man to People's President Born: October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, India Died: July 27, 2015, Shillong, India A life of science and service: Early life: Grew up in a humble family, overcame obstacles to pursue education in aerospace engineering. Scientific contributions: Played a pivotal role in India's missile and space programs, earning the title "Missile Man." Led projects like SLV-III and Agni missiles, Rohini satellite launch. Political journey: Served as Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister (1992-1999), oversaw Pokhran-II nuclear tests. Elected 11th President of India in 2002, known for his down-to-earth approach and focus on youth development. Legacy: Remembered for his scientific accomplishments, visionary leadership, and dedication to empowering youth. Earned the nickname "People's President" for his accessibility and connection with the common people. Key achievements: Led India's first satellite launch vehicle project (SLV-III) and developed the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV). Played a crucial role in developing Agni and Prithvi missiles, strengthening India's defense capabilities.

Biography

  • Born in 1931 in Rameswaram, India, to a humble Tamil Muslim family.
  • Overcame financial hardship and pursued his passion for aerospace engineering.
  • Graduated from Madras Institute of Technology in 1960
  • Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • Served as Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister (1992-1999).
